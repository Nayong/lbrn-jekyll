Research Presenter Guidelines

Below are the details for your oral presentations (please see the agenda for the assigned time) at the Annual Meeting on Saturday, Janurary 18, 2013. The Full and Project PI's are invited to give a 15 minute presentation highlighting the progress of their LBRN research project during the past year. As indicated, in the suggested presentation outline below, this presentation is different from a presentation normally made at a research meeting and must be designed to summarize your project and what has been acomplished during the past summer. After the presentations, the Pilot and Full Project PI's will have an opporitunity to meet with the LBRN EAC and the Summer Faculty and Graduate Students will have an opporitunity for Q&A's or feedback regarding their research project.

The research presentation should include the following:

  • Project Title (1 Slide)
    • Mentee
    • Name and Institution
    • Mentors
    • Name and Institution
  • Goals (1-2 Slides)
  • Overall Goals and Specific Aims of your project
  • Hypotheses (1 Slide)
  • Hypotheses investigated during the past 12 months
  • Summary: Experimental Approach/Results (2-3 Slides)
  • Brief summary of Experimental approach/es and results accomplished during the past 12 months
  • Impediments & Solutions (1 Slide)
  • Did you encounter any impediments in accomplishing your project? If so, what steps did you take to overcome this?
  • Next Steps (1-2 Slides)
  • What are the next steps in your research?

  • Project progress (1 Slide)
  • Summarize measurable accomplishments such as papers, abstracts, grants submitted or funded, and students trained.
  • Address how does this fit within the overall time line for the project.
  • Future (optional slides)
  • How your project will lead into the area of Clinical Translational Research and Community Based Participatory Research?
  • Acknowledgements (1 slide)
  • Acknowledgements

Summer Research Presenter Guidelines

Also, at the Annual Meeting the Summer Faculty and Graduate Students are to give a 15 minute presentation highlighting the progress of their LBRN research project during the past summer. As indicated in the suggested presentation outline below, this presentation is different from a presentation normally made at a research meeting and must be designed to summarize your project and what has been accomplished during the past summer. After the presentations, the Summer Faculty and Graduate Students will have an opportunity for Q&As or feedbacks regarding their research project.

The summer research presentation should include the following:

  • Project Title (1 Slide)
    • Mentee
    • Name and Institution
    • Mentors
    • Name and Institution
  • Goals (1 Slide)
  • Overall Goals and Specific Aims of your project
  • Hypotheses (1 Slide)
  • Hypotheses investigated during the summer
  • Summary: Experimental Approach/Results (2-4 Slides)
  • Brief summary of Experimental approach/es and results accomplished during the past summer
  • Next Steps & Project Progress (1 Slide)
  • What are the next steps in your research?
  • Summarize measurable accomplishments such as papers, abstracts, grants submitted or funded, and students trained.
  • Address how this fits within the overall time line for the project.
  • Acknowledgements (1 slide)
  • Acknowledgements

Note: In order for us to be on schedule, we ask that you do not exceed the number of slides listed above or the time allotted for your presentation since you will need time for questions and answers.

The deadline to have the slides to John Quebedeaux, johnq@lsu.edu, is Friday, January 18, 2013 at registration.

Presentation Requirements: Note type of platform and file type you are sending (i.e. Windows/Powerpoint, OS X/Powerpoint, OS X/Keynote, etc.) Include any images/movies/etc. into the file (i.e. make sure they are embedded if you referenced external files). Use the standard typefaces on your system. If you send them early enough, John takes the time to check how they look and will let you know if there are any problems.

The abstract cannot exceed 2000 characters. Abstracts for research presentations will be printed in the LBRN 11th Annual Meeting Program.


Deadline for oral abstract submission is Monday, January 7, 2013.

Covered Expenses

For LA Tech Employees
No reimbursement for hotel or mileage.
For Non-LA Tech Employees and Students
If driving personal vehicle:
Provide time/date leaving and returning to your residence/school. Also provide odometer beginning/end or mileage map calculation for mileage justification for a mileage reimbursement at 51 cents per mile.
Note for LSU employees: Mileage reimbursement not to exceed a maximum of 99 miles.
If driving a university owned vehicle (LSU Employees):
Please follow department's policies and procedures. If billing code is required, please contact Mrs. Terri Gilbert at tgilbert@lsu.edu.
If renting a vehicle:
First send Mrs. Terri Gilbert, tgilbert@lsu.edu, an email for request for vehicle rental for travel reimbursement purposes. Then contact your travel agent or Enterprise Rent-A-Car for vehicle rental and have it charged to your personal credit card. Insurance is not reimbursable for domestic travel. Keep all your receipts along with gas receipts for travel reimbursements request.
Vehicle Rental Size
Only the cost of rental of compact or mid-size/intermediate model is reimbursable, unless 1) non-availability is documented, 2) the vehicle will be used to transport more than two individuals.
Gasoline (receipts required)
Reimbursements require a receipt for regular unleaded gasoline, or diesel when applicable only to the driver renting the vehicle.
Insurance for Vehicle Rentals
Insurance billed by vehicle rental companies (i.e., CDW or LDW) is not reimbursable for domestic travel.

* Personal Accident Insurance, Supplemental Liability Protection and Roadside Assistance is not reimbursable.

Hotel:
Block rate for Louisiana Biomedical Research Network group will be covered (be sure to get registered for room in time for this rate and to reserve a room, otherwise the full amount can not be covered). Incidentals are the participant's responsibility.

Support from the National Institutes of Health and National Institute of General Medical Sciences 8P20GM103424 is gratefully acknowledged.
